What a Library Remembers

KnowBridge Podcast· with Dr. A. Morandi & L. Khoury
12:0438:00

An archive is not a neutral box of old paper. Every collection is a series of choices — what to keep, what to label, what to quietly let go. In this episode we ask what those choices mean for the past we think we remember.

We sit down with a historian and a translator to trace how a single document travels: from the desk it was written on, to the shelf that preserved it, to the catalogue that named it, to the reader who finally finds it — sometimes centuries later, sometimes never.

"What an archive forgets tells you as much as what it keeps."

It's a conversation about memory, power, and the very human work of deciding what counts as worth remembering — and how digitisation and AI are rewriting those rules right now.

In this episode

  • Why "saving everything" was never really an option
  • How language and translation shape what survives
  • The quiet politics of a catalogue
  • What changes when an archive goes digital
